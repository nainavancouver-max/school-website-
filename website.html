What Is This About?

Secularism is the act of separating religion from public relations. The aim of this project is to bring accountability to schools that claim to practice secularism. The grasp that Christianity has on our government system allows itself to weave its practices into classrooms, regardless of the intention.


An example of these ideologies seeping into supposedly neutral classroom settings can be directly found in our calendar system. Our mandatory school breaks revolve around Christian centred holidays such as Easter, Good Friday and Christmas. From this, teachers base classroom activities solely on these celebrations, only promoting select religious activities.

×

Why Does It Matter?

Lacking in secularism and favouring one religion negatively impacts students who either practice different religions or none at all. While focusing on the celebrations of one religion individuals may feel a loss of identity and/or alienated in a predominately Christian setting.


Through this division many students may feel lack of representation causing linear thinking and forcing people to confine themselves into a single standard—in this context the western “standard”.

×

What Can We Do?

Realistically it would be too complex to add all religions into the current education system. Therefore the most realistic solution is to enforce secularism entirely with no double standards.


This means removing the discussion of religion entirely and keeping it within private households. If teachers choose to participate in religious holiday activities, it is crucial to not concentrate on a singular belief.


Creating a genuine neutral space will allow students to focus on meaningful connections without learned biases, while also allowing minorities to have a presence within the space they grow up in.
